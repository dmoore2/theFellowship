Hello Class of 2016!



We are a a fellowship of involved and dedicated juniors with very different interests and backgrounds. We six have united for one ultimate mission: to improve the Stanford experience of our class. As a brief introduction, here are the key issues that we all are passionate about and have decided focus on in our agenda.

1) Increasing the number of students that can go abroad.

Current programs aren't as big as the student populace that is qualified and interested in participating, and many majors involve stringent requirements that make it hard to go abroad during any academic year. We are interested in working with Bing Overseas and the Haas Center to increase the number of students that can attend current overseas studies programs, as well as introduce a well-established and Stanford-associated program for working abroad during the summer.

2) Consolidating mental health awareness and support.

We will work to form a committee composed of members of all mental health groups. This will help the junior class work through the pressures of approaching graduation by providing a place for students to go for support and health counseling.

3) Establishing a career mentorship network on campus.

Our slate has a passion for creating mentorship and education and one of our focuses will be to connect juniors with professionals in their fields of interest. We are well aware that for many of us, our major doesn't constitute our desired career, and would like to start a series of mixers and academic talks to help students explore and discover their interests. Speakers can range from tech to modern art to public policy- whatever our class thinks we need most.

4) Make the arts more accessible to students. We want art to play a more central role on campus.

We plan to address this issue by increasing the number of openings in classes such as studio art classes - so students can explore passions outside their majors. We are planning to work with Cantor to hold more student-oriented events to encourage participation in and appreciation for the arts on campus. If there is a significant amount of student interest, a junior class musical could also be fun.

5) Pushing campus cultural diversity programming.

While Stanford is home to one of the most diverse student populations in the United States, we have yet to exploit that in cultural programming. A junior class culture show (that is free admission) and class events with plenty of ethnic foods are only a couple ideas we have for accomplishing this.

6) And, last but not least... planning fun social events for juniors!

What kind of parties you ask? Think... Wolf of Wall Street... Most importantly, we are here for you Class of 2016. Really, that is why we are doing this. We want to win this so much because we truly believe that our slate has the dedication, the enthusiasm, and the spirit to make Junior year an amazing experience. We have been planning this campaign for a long time and are willing to go the extra mile for each of you, so please, contact us. We want to get to know you! Please ask us questions about our agenda and let us know if you think that something is missing on it. Our goal is to make Junior year as enriching, diverse, stirring, and memorable experience as possible.
